Class implementing the Composition Function. #lt;br#gt; The composition function supports an on-the-fly, bounded composition. #lt;br#gt; It is possible to invoke a composition stopping at a given depth, and invoking again at a greater depth. #lt;br#gt; In this case when reapplying the function, the previous states are stored and will not be generated again. #lt;br#gt;   This composition is a special type of synchronous product where synchronizations (called matches) are not broadcast, i.e., they only involve two principals. #lt;br#gt; The arguments of the composition may be automata having rank greater than 1, i.e., representing an ensemble of composed automata. #lt;br#gt; In this case, pre-existing matches inside the operands automata are preserved and are not rearranged. #lt;br#gt; By changing the order in which principal automata are composed, different results can be obtained, in other words, this composition is non-associative. #lt;br#gt; The associative composition is a special case where all operands are of rank 1.#lt;br#gt; The formal definition of this composition is specified in Definition 5 of<br/><ul>
<li> Basile, D. et al., 2020. Controller synthesis of service contracts with variability. Science of Computer Programming, vol. 187, pp. 102344. (<a href="file://https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scico.2019.102344">https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scico.2019.102344</a>)<br/></li></ul> <br/>@param #lt;S1#gt; the generic type of the content of states<br/>@param #lt;S#gt; the generic type of states, must be a subtype of #lt;code#gt;State#lt;S1#gt;#lt;/code#gt;<br/>@param #lt;L#gt; the generic type of the labels, must be a subtype of #lt;code#gt;Label#lt;Action#gt;#lt;/code#gt;<br/>@param #lt;T#gt; the generic type of a transitions, must be a subtype of #lt;code#gt;ModalTransition#lt;S1,Action,S,L#gt;#lt;/code#gt;<br/>@param #lt;A#gt; the generic type of the automata, must be a subtype of #lt;code#gt;Automaton#lt;S1,Action,S,T #gt;#lt;/code#gt; <br/><br/>
